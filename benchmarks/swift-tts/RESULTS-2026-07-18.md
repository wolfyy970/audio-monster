# Native Swift TTS results — M3 MacBook Air

These results were produced with Blaizzy's `mlx-audio-swift` at commit
`542fffacb3be8de47024b3b54888f71d72d46d30`, using the same 32-word narration
fixture for every model. Each model ran in a separate process: one cold generation
followed by three warm generations. The table reports the warm median.

| Model | Cached load | Warm speed | First audio | Duration spread | Peak RSS | Installed model data | Voices / role |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| **Kokoro 82M bf16** | **0.23 s** | **6.63× realtime** | 2.28 s | **0.00 s** | 480 MB | 354 MB incl. English G2P | **54 voices; recommended** |
| Soprano 80M bf16 | 0.43 s | 4.95× realtime | 2.12 s | 1.98 s | 316 MB | 210 MB | One voice; speed baseline only |
| Pocket TTS | 0.29 s | 2.21× realtime | 5.06 s | 0.48 s | 325 MB | 229 MB | Eight voices |
| Qwen3 TTS 0.6B 8-bit | 2.91 s | 0.79× realtime | **0.58 s** | 2.00 s | 1,028 MB | 1.9 GB | Two English presets; cloning capable |
| Marvis 250M 8-bit | 0.84 s | 0.66× realtime | 0.93 s | 1.20 s | 1,111 MB | ~1.0 GB incl. Mimi codec | Two English voices |

“First audio” is the median warm time to the first non-empty chunk. Kokoro, Pocket,
and Soprano currently emit only completed audio, so their first-audio time equals the
generation time. Qwen and Marvis genuinely stream chunks, but neither sustained
real-time generation in this test.

## Recommendation

Use native Swift Kokoro as Audio Monster's default engine.

- It is 8.4 times the throughput of Qwen and 10 times Marvis in this test.
- It retains the product's useful voice catalogue: 54 voices across nine languages.
- It had zero output-duration variation across all four runs, no clipped samples, and
  no non-finite samples.
- Its cached load was 0.23 seconds and its installed data was about one sixth of Qwen's.
- It removes the embedded Python/FastAPI service from the desktop synthesis path.

The Kokoro Swift implementation downloads `beshkenadze/kitten-tts-g2p` for English
grapheme-to-phoneme conversion. Despite that repository name, it is a small text
processor dependency, not the excluded Kitten TTS synthesizer or voice model.

For responsive playback and accurate progress, split article text into the model's
recommended 100–200-token chunks. Generate those sequentially through one shared MLX
model instance, append each completed chunk to playback/storage, and report progress
from completed text/token counts. Running several model instances concurrently on an
M3's single integrated GPU would contend for the same GPU and unified memory rather
than use separate GPUs.

## Representative artifacts

The benchmark command generates cold and warm WAVs locally under
`results/2026-07-18/`. These outputs are intentionally ignored: they are large,
reproducible artifacts rather than source, and generated speech can carry model-specific
distribution terms.

To inspect slowed speech with Apple's high-quality rate converter:

```sh
afplay -r 0.4 -q 1 results/2026-07-18/kokoro/kokoro-warm-3.wav
```

Raw JSON and all cold/warm WAVs are written to `results/2026-07-18/` (ignored by
source control because model runs are generated artifacts).
